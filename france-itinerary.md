# Paris in 4.5 Days — First-Timer's Itinerary (Home Base: Bastille)

Staying by Bastille is a great call for a first visit: you're in a real, lived-in neighborhood with some of the best casual food and nightlife in the city, and you're on three metro lines (1, 5, 8) that reach almost everything. Line 1 alone gets you to the Marais, the Louvre, the Champs-Élysées, and La Défense.

**Assumption:** you land with a half day first, then have 4 full days. If your half day is at the end instead, just flip Day 0 to the last afternoon.

---

## Before You Go — Quick Logistics

- **Metro:** Get a Navigo Easy card at any station and load a carnet (10 tickets) — cheaper than singles. Or use the Île-de-France Mobilités app to buy tickets on your phone.
- **Book ahead (essential, they sell out):** Louvre timed entry, Eiffel Tower summit tickets, Sainte-Chapelle, and Versailles if you go. Musée d'Orsay is worth booking too.
- **Museum tip:** Most museums close Monday or Tuesday — Orsay and Versailles close **Monday**, the Louvre closes **Tuesday**. Check before locking days in.
- **Eating rhythm:** Lunch ~12:00–14:00, dinner from 19:30. Kitchens genuinely close between services — plan around it or grab a bakery.
- **Tipping:** Not expected. Round up or leave a couple of euros for great service.
- **Basic courtesy:** Always open with "Bonjour" before asking anything. It changes every interaction.

## Day 0 (Half Day) — Settle Into Your Neighborhood

Keep it low-effort and walkable — you'll be tired.

- **Place de la Bastille** — stand where the prison stood (the July Column marks it), get oriented.
- **Coulée Verte (Promenade Plantée)** — the elevated park built on an old rail viaduct, starting just behind Bastille on Avenue Daumesnil. This was the inspiration for New York's High Line, and it's blissfully tourist-free. Walk 30–45 minutes of it.
- **Viaduc des Arts** — under the Coulée Verte, artisan workshops in the brick arches.
- **Port de l'Arsenal** — the little marina between Bastille and the Seine. Nice sunset spot.
- **Dinner on Rue de Lappe / Rue de la Roquette** — the streets just off Bastille are packed with bistros and bars. For a proper first meal, try **Bistrot Paul Bert** (11 rue Paul Bert, book ahead — one of the classic Paris bistros, 15 min walk) or keep it casual with a crêpe or steak-frites anywhere that looks busy with locals.

## Day 1 — The Heart of Paris: Islands, Latin Quarter, Marais

All walkable from your door. No metro needed until you're tired.

**Morning**
- Walk 15 minutes west along Rue Saint-Antoine into the **Marais**. Detour into **Place des Vosges** — the oldest planned square in Paris, and arguably its most beautiful. Victor Hugo's house (free) is on the corner.
- Continue to **Île Saint-Louis**, the quieter island. Get a **Berthillon** ice cream even if it's 10am.
- Cross to **Île de la Cité**: **Notre-Dame** (reopened after the fire — free entry, but reserve a time slot online to skip the line) and **Sainte-Chapelle** (book ahead — the stained glass is the single most jaw-dropping interior in Paris).

**Afternoon**
- Cross to the Left Bank into the **Latin Quarter**: Shakespeare and Company bookstore, the Panthéon, wander the medieval streets around Rue Mouffetard.
- Coffee break in the **Jardin du Luxembourg** — grab a green chair by the fountain like a local.

**Evening**
- Dinner in the Latin Quarter or Saint-Germain, or head back toward home. **Le Baron Rouge** (near Marché d'Aligre, 10 min from Bastille) is a beloved standing wine bar — oysters and a glass of wine in a barrel-lined room.

## Day 2 — The Louvre & the Grand Axis

**Morning**
- Metro Line 1 from Bastille → Palais Royal–Musée du Louvre (10 min). Enter at your **booked time slot**, ideally the 9:00 opening.
- Don't try to see it all. Pick a wing and 2–3 hours: Mona Lisa + Italian paintings, Winged Victory, Venus de Milo, the Napoleon III apartments. Leave while you still like museums.
- Decompress in the **Jardin des Tuileries** next door.

**Afternoon**
- Walk the **grand axis**: Tuileries → **Place de la Concorde** → up the **Champs-Élysées** (honestly skippable as a shopping street, but the walk is iconic) → **Arc de Triomphe**. Climb it — many people prefer this view to the Eiffel Tower's because the Eiffel Tower is *in* it.

**Evening**
- Metro to **Trocadéro** for the classic head-on Eiffel Tower view, then walk down and across the river to the tower itself. If you booked summit tickets, go up around sunset. Either way, stay for the **sparkle** — the tower glitters for 5 minutes at the top of every hour after dark.
- Dinner near there is touristy; consider riding Line 8 straight back to Bastille and eating in your neighborhood instead.

## Day 3 — Orsay, Saint-Germain & Montmartre

**Morning**
- **Musée d'Orsay** at opening (book ahead; closed Mondays). The Impressionist floor — Monet, Van Gogh, Renoir, Degas — is for most people more enjoyable than the Louvre. 2–3 hours, and don't miss the giant clock window.

**Midday**
- Wander **Saint-Germain-des-Prés**: the historic cafés (Café de Flore / Les Deux Magots — pay the tourist premium for one coffee, it's worth doing once), Rue de Buci market street for lunch.

**Afternoon → Evening**
- Metro up to **Montmartre** (Line 12 to Abbesses). Explore the hilltop village: Place du Tertre (touristy but fun), the quiet streets behind it — Rue de l'Abreuvoir and the Maison Rose, the vineyards, Sacré-Cœur and its terrace view over the whole city. Best in late afternoon light, and sunset from the steps is a classic.
- Dinner in Montmartre (book something — good spots fill up) or back home to Bastille.

## Day 4 — Pick Your Finale

Three good options depending on your taste:

**Option A — Versailles (the classic day trip)**
- RER C or the train from Montparnasse to Versailles (~45–60 min door to door). Book **Passport tickets with timed palace entry** well ahead; go early.
- Palace in the morning (Hall of Mirrors), then the real magic: the **gardens**, the Grand Trianon, and **Marie Antoinette's hamlet**. Rent bikes or a golf cart for the grounds — they're enormous. This is a full day.

**Option B — Deep-cut Paris day (no trains, more soul)**
- Morning: **Marché d'Aligre** (your local market, 10 min away — one of the best in Paris, closed Mondays) for breakfast and browsing.
- **Père Lachaise cemetery** (short ride on Line 2 or a 25-min walk) — Jim Morrison, Oscar Wilde, Chopin, and the most atmospheric strolling in the city.
- Afternoon: **Canal Saint-Martin** — walk the locks and iron footbridges, coffee and vintage shops; the canal actually flows underground from the Bastille marina, so you can walk there from home via Boulevard Richard Lenoir.
- Evening: **Centre Pompidou area** and a last Marais wander, falafel on **Rue des Rosiers** (L'As du Fallafel — the line moves fast).

**Option C — Seine day**
- Morning at the **Rodin Museum** (sculpture garden is lovely) + **Les Invalides** (Napoleon's tomb).
- Afternoon **Seine river cruise** (Vedettes du Pont Neuf is a good no-frills one) — cheesy, but genuinely one of the best ways to see the city, especially if your feet are done.
- Sunset drinks along the **Rives de Seine** riverbank park.

## Bastille Home-Base Cheat Sheet

**Eat & drink near you**
- **Marché d'Aligre** — daily market (except Mon), 10 min walk. Go at least once.
- **Le Baron Rouge** — standing wine bar by the market. A Paris institution.
- **Bistrot Paul Bert** — the quintessential bistro. Book days ahead. Steak au poivre + the soufflé.
- **Septime / Clamato** (Rue de Charonne) — if you want one splurge meal; Septime needs booking weeks out, Clamato (seafood, no reservations) is the walk-in alternative.
- **Rue de Lappe / Rue de Charonne / Rue Keller** — bar and bistro streets right off the square.
- **Blé Sucré** (Square Trousseau) — get the croissant and the madeleine. Among the best in Paris, 8 min from you.

**Your metro lines**
- **Line 1** → Marais (St-Paul), Louvre, Concorde, Champs-Élysées, Arc de Triomphe
- **Line 8** → Opéra, Invalides, École Militaire (Eiffel Tower)
- **Line 5** → Gare du Nord (Eurostar/airport RER connections), République

**Walking times from Bastille**
- Place des Vosges: 10 min · Île Saint-Louis: 15 min · Notre-Dame: 20 min · Marché d'Aligre: 10 min · Canal Saint-Martin: 20 min via Bd Richard Lenoir
